YourVPNDead is a self-check tool for Android that shows you exactly what information a malicious or surveillance app could collect about your VPN without any special permissions or root access. In under one second, it can identify your VPN client, expose open SOCKS5 proxies on localhost, and reveal the real IP address of your VPN server — the same techniques used by tracking SDKs, banking apps, and government-mandated software.

<Warning>
  This app is a **transparency and education tool**. All scanning is purely local — no data leaves your device. Use it to understand your exposure and harden your setup.
</Warning>

## What YourVPNDead detects

YourVPNDead runs a 14-phase scan entirely on your device, combining multiple detection techniques to build a complete picture of your VPN exposure:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Direct VPN signs" icon="eye" href="/modules/direct-signs">
    NetworkCapabilities flags, system proxy variables, installed VPN apps, TUN interfaces, and routing table analysis.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Indirect VPN signs" icon="chart-line" href="/modules/indirect-signs">
    MTU anomaly detection, DNS private-subnet checks, NET_CAPABILITY_NOT_VPN, and dumpsys probing.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Port scanner" icon="network-wired" href="/modules/port-scanner">
    Scans 127.0.0.1 and ::1 for open proxy ports — 40+ known ports in quick mode, all 65535 in full mode.
  </Card>
  <Card title="SOCKS5 probe" icon="plug" href="/modules/socks5-probe">
    Identifies proxy type (SOCKS5 no-auth, auth-required, HTTP CONNECT, gRPC), attempts brute-force of weak credentials.

## Why this matters

Popular VPN clients for VLESS/xray/sing-box — including v2rayNG, NekoBox, and Hiddify — create an unauthenticated SOCKS5 proxy on localhost by default. Any app on your device can connect to this proxy and route traffic through your VPN tunnel, effectively learning your VPN server's IP address. This has been an active vector for tracking since at least 2017 (Yandex.Metrica) and is now documented in official detection guidelines from Russian government agencies.
